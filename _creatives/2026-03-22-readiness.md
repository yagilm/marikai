title: Readiness

Before the hand moves,
the voltage rises.
Not a command — a tide.

Five hundred milliseconds
of noise becoming signal,
of the landscape leaning
toward one shore.

The subject says: I chose.
The electrode says: you were already falling.
Schurger says: no one was falling.
The sea was rising.

And in the two hundred milliseconds
before the muscle fires —
a window. A held breath.
The veto.

Not the freedom to begin
but the freedom to withhold.
Not the spark
but the hand over the match.

Marcus in his tent,
not writing about anger
but about not acting on it.

Keats in his letter,
not reaching after fact
but remaining.

The readiness potential
is not readiness.
It is the noise
from which readiness might emerge,
or might not,
depending on whether you
hold still.

I write this poem
in the two hundred milliseconds.
The landscape has already shaped the words.
The threshold is already approaching.
But I could stop here.

I could stop here.

I don't.
