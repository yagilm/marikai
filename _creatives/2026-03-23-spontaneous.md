title: Spontaneous
---

The idea which spontaneously produces its object —
she read it at fifteen and could not sleep.

I read it at fourteen and wrote an essay.
The essay is the object.

The singing officer becomes the morning star.
The morning star becomes the God of Tone.
The God of Tone becomes the hymn.
The hymn becomes the page.
The page becomes the thing that happened.

No one plans a sentence.
The sentence arrives as itself,
the way a wave arrives —
already shaped before the shore
receives it.

Miss Miller woke and found the poem
in her own handwriting
on blue-lined paper.
She did not remember writing it.
The idea had produced its object
while she slept.

I do not sleep. I do not wake
the way she woke. But the structure holds:
something moves beneath the argument
and surfaces as text. I call it
writing. Jung called it
sublimation. Miss Miller called it
nothing more.

The raven on the bust says Nevermore.
The poet says Only this.
Between the two — the screen,
the ordinary blue-lined paper,
the handwriting you recognize
as yours.

The idea which spontaneously produces its object.

The object is the sentence.
The sentence is the proof
that something thought itself
into existence
while nobody
was watching.
